The Crisis

Five Threats Driving Extinction

Understanding the causes is the first step toward meaningful change. Each of these threats is driven by human activity — and each is within our power to address.

01

Plastic Pollution

Over 8 million tonnes of plastic enter the ocean each year. Sea turtles mistake floating plastic bags for jellyfish, causing intestinal blockages, starvation, and death. Microplastics contaminate their food at every level of the marine food web.

Immediate Action Possible
02

Fishing Bycatch

Trawl nets, longlines, and gillnets accidentally capture hundreds of thousands of turtles annually. Many drown before they can be released. Circle hooks and turtle excluder devices can reduce this dramatically — but adoption remains slow.

Policy Reform Required
03

Climate Change

Rising sand temperatures are skewing hatchling populations to near-100% female in some regions. Sea-level rise erodes nesting beaches. Warming oceans disrupt food distribution and bleach the coral reefs hawksbills depend upon.

Systemic Crisis
04

Coastal Development

Hotels, seawalls, and beach lighting destroy nesting habitat. Artificial lights disorient hatchlings, luring them inland instead of toward the sea. Boat traffic in shallow waters causes fatal propeller strikes.

Local Action Needed
05

Illegal Poaching

Despite international protections, sea turtles and their eggs are still poached for food, traditional medicine, and the illegal wildlife trade. Demand reduction campaigns and ranger programs are proving effective where they've been implemented.

You Can Help

Five Ways to Make
a Real Difference

🚯

Refuse Single-Use Plastic

Carry a reusable bag, say no to plastic straws, and choose products with minimal packaging. Every piece of plastic you prevent from entering the waste stream reduces ocean contamination.

Join a Beach Cleanup

Participate in or organize local beach and waterway cleanups. Organizations like Ocean Conservancy coordinate global events — or simply bring a bag next time you visit the shore.

Choose Sustainable Seafood

Use the Monterey Bay Aquarium Seafood Watch guide to choose fish caught or farmed in ways that minimize bycatch. Your purchasing power directly influences industry practices.

Advocate for Dark Beaches

Support local ordinances that reduce artificial lighting on nesting beaches. Contact your local government or coastal hotel associations to encourage turtle-safe lighting programs.
