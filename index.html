Why Turtles Fascinate Us

Survivors of mass extinctions, navigators of the open ocean, and symbols of longevity.

⏳

Living Fossils

Sea turtles have existed for more than 100 million years — outlasting the dinosaurs. Their body plan has remained remarkably unchanged throughout deep time.

🧭

Natural GPS

Sea turtles navigate the globe using Earth's magnetic field. Females return to the exact beach where they were born — sometimes traveling thousands of miles to do so.

🌊

Ocean Architects

Turtles are keystone species. They maintain healthy seagrass beds and coral reefs, support marine food webs, and cycle nutrients from sea to shore.

🥚

Beach Nurseries

Females crawl ashore at night to deposit up to 100 eggs in warm sand. After 60 days, hatchlings emerge and dash toward the moonlit ocean.

🫁

Breath-Holders

Sea turtles can hold their breath for up to 7 hours while resting. Their heart rate slows to just one beat per minute in a state of underwater dormancy.

🧬

Heat-Based Gender

A turtle's sex is determined by the temperature of its nest. Warmer sands produce females, cooler sands produce males — making them especially vulnerable to climate change.
